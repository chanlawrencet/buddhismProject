Boddhisattva Avalokitesvara
[n.d.]. Boddhisattva Avalokitesvara. http://library.artstor.org/asset/ARTSTOR_103_41822000816007.

Tang Dynasty

26.4 cm

University of California, San Diego

This Boddhisattva Avalokitesvara stands about 26.4 cm tall and seems to be meant to placed on a ledge. Their left leg hangs over as their right leg, bent in, is on the surface. The sculpture, made of a stone-like material is quite adorned. Bead strings run all over their body, and they seem to be holding a snake (or rope) in their right hand.

The Boddhisattva is faintly smiling, and has their head at a bit of a tilt. With beads running across their thin body, they seem very relaxed sitting. No shoes are available, and the Boddhisattva also has a headdress on. It is unknown whether the sculputre is just black and the whites are reflections, or that the picture is simply in black and white, making the sculpture appear that way.
